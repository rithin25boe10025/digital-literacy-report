### <a name="_h6lxu0f24ivo"></a>**'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for College Students**
Maintaining a secure digital presence is essential, especially with the increasing integration of academic and financial life online. Here is a specific, actionable checklist tailored for students in India:

1. **Enable App-Based 2FA:** Activate Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your university portals, Gmail, and social media. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) instead of SMS to prevent "SIM swapping" attacks.
1. **Audit Social Privacy:** Set your Instagram and Facebook profiles to 'Private.' Regularly review your followers and remove any 'ghost' accounts or individuals you do not know personally.
1. **The UPI PIN Golden Rule:** Never enter your UPI PIN to *receive* money. Scammers often send "Request Money" notifications disguised as refunds or scholarship payments. Remember: A PIN is only for sending.
1. **Verify UPI Identities:** Before hitting 'Pay' on apps like PhonePe or Google Pay, double-check the verified name of the recipient. Scammers often use names similar to official organizations to trick students.
1. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or student portal using free Wi-Fi at cafes or railway stations. If necessary, use a reputable VPN or your mobile hotspot.
1. **Spot "Easy Money" Scams:** Be extremely wary of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering "Part-time Work-from-Home" jobs that involve liking YouTube videos or making small "investments." These are often sophisticated financial mule scams.
1. **Reverse Image Search:** If a new "student" or recruiter contacts you with a profile picture that looks like a stock photo, use Google Lens to check if the image is stolen from elsewhere.
1. **Revoke Third-Party Access:** Every semester, go to your Google or LinkedIn security settings and "Sign out" of old apps or websites you no longer use (e.g., old gaming sites or quiz apps).
1. **Secure Your Webcam:** Use a physical slider or a small piece of tape over your laptop’s camera when not in use to prevent "camfecting" by malicious software.
