w**hat is cyberbullying**:

` `**cyberbullying** is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets. It is a form of harassment that uses the internet or telecommunications to target a specific individual with the intent to harm, embarrass, or isolate them.

**The Incident, Sofia’s Social Media Shadow:**

Sofia, a 15-year-old high school student, became the target of a coordinated cyberbullying campaign that escalated from minor teasing to a serious legal matter.
### <a name="_82si8iaofxvg"></a>**How It Happened (Step-by-Step):**
1. **Initial Contact:** Classmates began leaving derogatory comments on Sofia’s public photos, targeting her physical appearance (body shaming).
1. **Escalation (Identity Theft):** The bullies created a **"fraping" or fake account** using Sofia’s name and profile picture.
1. **Dissemination:** From this fake account, they posted fabricated, scandalous status updates and sent offensive messages to her friends to isolate her.
1. **Mob Mentality:** Other students, seeing the viral nature of the posts, joined in by sharing the "evidence" and adding further insults, a phenomenon known as **dogpiling**.
### <a name="_flswvbgkfomo"></a>**Who Was Targeted**
While anyone can be a victim, research shows that **older teenage girls** (ages 15–17) and **LGBTQ+ youth** are disproportionately targeted.

**Consequences**
### <a name="_yjjb8xkzln9s"></a>**1. For the Victim**
The impact is often more intense than traditional bullying because the harassment is relentless (24/7) and has a massive potential audience.

- **Mental & Emotional:** Severe anxiety, depression, and a loss of self-esteem. In extreme cases, this leads to self-harm or suicidal ideation.
- **Physical:** Stress-related symptoms like chronic headaches, stomachaches, and persistent insomnia.
- **Academic:** Sudden drops in grades, loss of concentration, or "school refusal" (avoiding school to escape bullies they know in real life).
- **Social:** Victims often withdraw from friends and family, leading to deep isolation.
### <a name="_fvh6ywm3r2s6"></a>**2. For the Perpetrator (Legal & Social)**
Even if an attack is meant as a "joke," the legal system increasingly treats cyberbullying as a serious crime. In **India**, for example, under the **IT Act (2000)** and the **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023**:

- **Imprisonment:** Crimes like **stalking (Section 78 BNS)** or **identity theft (Section 66C IT Act)** can lead to up to **3 years in prison**.
- **Fines:** Penalties for publishing obscene material or violating privacy can reach up to **₹5 lakh** ($500,000$ INR) or more for repeat offenses.
- **Permanent Record:** A criminal conviction can result in being barred from certain universities, losing job opportunities, or being denied travel visas.
