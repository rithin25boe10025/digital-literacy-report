### <a name="_id7keo4tkvj7"></a>**Social Media Best Practices for College Students**

|**Do**|**Don't**|
| :- | :- |
|**Audit your privacy settings** regularly to ensure you know exactly who can see your posts and tagged photos.|**Post during class or lectures.** It can come across as disrespectful to faculty and distracting to peers.|
|**Network intentionally** by following industry leaders, alumni, and organizations related to your major.|**Engage in "keyboard warrior" behavior.** Heated public arguments can resurface years later during background checks.|
|**Highlight your achievements,** such as internship wins, volunteer work, or club projects, to build a digital portfolio.|**Share confidential information** about your university, research projects, or part-time job.|
|**Think before you tag.** Ask friends for permission before posting photos of them, and expect the same in return.|**Post "venting" sessions** about specific professors or difficult courses; it’s a small world and word travels fast.|
|**Verify information** before sharing or reposting to avoid spreading misinformation or "fake news."|**Assume "Delete" means it's gone.** Screenshots are forever, even if the original post was only up for seconds.|
